1 · Load

The order file needs: article number, title, order quantity and package dimensions (W×H×D). Anything with missing sizes is held aside — never guessed. Category matrix (Matrix_Shelves.xlsx) extends the article→category map; Top100 (Top100_regulare items.xlsx) flags best-sellers by EAN / article for eye-level priority.

2 · Fixture

Defaults from the measured store fixture: 76.2 cm usable width, 5 levels at 33.4 / 36 / 36 / 36 / 34 cm clear height, 197.1 cm total incl. 7 cm plinth. Depth is not in the front-view diagram — 40 cm assumed, confirm with Nina (it drives backstock depth).

3 · Verify

Merchandising rules applied

• Facings driven by order quantity (more ordered → more front columns)
• Front variety first: every product gets a facing, remaining units go behind as backstock
• Eye-level priority for top-sellers, larger and higher-value items; smaller/cheaper lower
• Left→right by shelf category (matrix order), then by article number
• No more than two identical designs side by side
• Shelf-space utilization reported per level and in total
