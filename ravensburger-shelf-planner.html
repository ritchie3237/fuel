1 · Load data

The order file needs: article number, title, order quantity and package dimensions (W×H×D). Missing sizes are held aside — never guessed. Photos embedded in the .xlsx load automatically; Load images matches photo files by article number in the filename. Matrix / Top100 buttons refresh the built-in category map and best-seller list with newer exports.

2 · Shelf templates

The 80 cm fixture is measured (Nina's photo): 76.2 cm usable, levels 33.4 / 36 / 36 / 36 / 34 cm top→bottom, 197.1 cm incl. 7 cm plinth. Depth (40 cm) and the 100 cm bay's inner sizes are assumed — confirm with Nina.

3 · Store shelves & theme areas

Number the store's shelves, give each a fixture template and a theme area (e.g. shelves 1–7 = BRIO Flora). The planner lays each theme out on exactly its own shelves — anything that doesn't fit is reported, not squeezed.

4 · Assign products

Tick the products to display in this theme area. Per product: view (front / side / unboxed demo unit), ★ featured (eye level + up to 4 facings), and optional pinning to a specific shelf or level. Held-aside items need sizes first.

Merchandising rules applied

• Facings follow order quantity, capped at 2 per product (4 for ★ featured / Top-100) for front variety
• Remaining units stack behind each facing as backstock (shelf depth ÷ package depth)
• Eye-level priority (≈140 cm) for featured, top-selling, larger and higher-value items
• Left→right by shelf category (matrix order), then article number, flowing across the theme's shelves
• No more than two identical designs side by side
• Unboxed demo units take one spot, no backstock
• Utilization reported per level and per theme area
